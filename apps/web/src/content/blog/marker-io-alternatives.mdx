**TL;DR:** Marker.io dropped its free plan entirely - you get a 15-day trial and that's it. After that, the cheapest option is the Starter plan at **$59/mo** ($39/mo if you commit to annual billing), capped at **3 seats, 1 project, and 5,000 pageviews/month**. Need more people? Extra seats run $4/mo each on annual billing. That's a lot of tax for a visual bug-reporting widget. If you're a dev team or agency that wants full technical context (console logs, network requests, environment data) without the per-seat squeeze - or you want to self-host the whole thing - there are cheaper, more dev-friendly marker.io alternatives. One of them, Faster Fixes, is open-source and free to run on your own infrastructure.

This post breaks down 8 marker.io alternatives, what they actually cost, and when each one makes sense.

## 1. Faster Fixes

**TL;DR:** Open-source, self-hostable visual feedback and bug-reporting tool built for developers - free tier included, plus an MCP server so Claude Code or Cursor can act on bug reports directly.

**Pricing:**
- Free plan - unlimited core usage for small teams
- $20/mo per 5 users - for growing teams
- $99/mo - for larger orgs, all features unlocked
- Self-host it yourself for $0, forever

**Best for:** Dev teams and agencies who want full technical context on every bug report (console logs, network requests, session replay, environment metadata) without paying a per-seat tax, and who might want an AI coding agent to pick up issues automatically.

**Strengths:**
- Actually has a free plan - not a trial, a real free tier
- Open-source (AGPL-3.0), so you can audit the code and self-host on your own servers
- MCP server integration lets Claude Code, Cursor, and other AI coding agents read and act on bug reports
- No pageview caps or "fair use" restrictions to worry about when self-hosting
- Pricing scales in flat per-5-user blocks instead of Marker.io's per-seat model

**Weaknesses:**
- Newer product, smaller integration marketplace than a 10-year-old incumbent like Marker.io
- Self-hosting requires you to manage your own infra, though it's still fairly straightforward - the [self-hosting guide](/docs/self-hosting) walks you through it

See the full breakdown on [pricing](/pricing) and what self-hosting actually involves on the [open-source](/open-source) page.

## 2. BugHerd

**TL;DR:** A well-known visual feedback tool for marketing sites and client projects, but pricing climbs fast once you add teammates.

**Pricing:**
- From $50/mo for 5 members
- Additional seats at $8/seat
- No free plan - trial only

**Best for:** Marketing and QA teams that need simple point-and-click feedback on websites without deep dev-tool integration.

**Strengths:**
- Simple, familiar Trello-like Kanban board for managing feedback
- Easy client onboarding - no account needed to leave feedback
- Solid browser extension for capturing issues on any site

**Weaknesses:**
- Per-seat pricing adds up quickly for teams past 5 people
- No free plan, so you're committing before you've fully tested it at scale
- Less technical context captured per bug than dev-first tools

Full side-by-side numbers are in our [BugHerd comparison](/vs/bugherd).

## 9. Marker.io

**TL;DR:** The tool most people are looking to replace when they search "marker.io alternatives" - solid dev-tool integrations, but no free plan and a pricing structure that punishes growing teams.

**Pricing:**
- $59/mo Starter (or $39/mo billed annually) - 3 seats, 1 project, 5,000 pageviews/month
- $199/mo Team (or $149/mo billed annually) - 15 seats, 3 projects, 25,000 pageviews/month
- $129/mo Agency (or $99/mo billed annually) - 15 seats, 50 projects, application required
- No free plan - 15-day free trial only, capped at 25 issues during the trial
- Extra seats: $4/mo (Starter) or $6/mo (Team), billed annually

**Best for:** Agencies and dev teams already committed to Jira, Trello, Linear, or GitHub workflows who need visual bug reports with session replay and don't mind the per-seat and pageview caps.

**Strengths:**
- Deep integrations with Jira, Linear, GitHub, GitLab, Azure DevOps, Trello, Asana, and more
- Session replay and detailed environment metadata on every issue
- Established product with a large customer base (L'Oréal, Vimeo, Expedia are cited case studies)
- Clean widget and browser extension setup

**Weaknesses:**
- No free plan at all - just a 15-day trial capped at 25 issues
- Starter plan's 5,000 pageview/month cap is easy to blow through - the widget counts every visitor session, not just people who submit feedback
- 1-project limit on Starter forces an upgrade almost immediately for teams managing more than one site
- Extra seats and extra projects both cost more on top of an already-steep base price
- No self-hosting option - you're fully dependent on their infrastructure and fair-use policy enforcement

If you're evaluating whether to stick with Marker.io or switch, our full [Marker.io comparison](/vs/marker-io) walks through the pricing math side by side with concrete team-size examples.

## How to choose

**If you're a dev-heavy team or agency wanting full technical context and no per-seat tax**, use Faster Fixes. You get console logs, network requests, and environment metadata without watching your bill climb every time you add a teammate - and the open-source option means you can run it entirely on your own infrastructure if that matters for compliance or cost control.

**If you're already deep in Jira, Linear, or GitHub workflows and don't mind the price**, Marker.io's dev-tool integrations are genuinely strong - that's the case for a lot of teams who end up on it despite the cost. Our [Marker.io comparison](/vs/marker-io) breaks down exactly when that trade-off is worth it versus when it isn't.

**If you're a marketing or QA team running client review cycles on a handful of sites**, BugHerd or Atarim's client-facing commenting tools are built for that specific workflow - just budget for per-seat costs as the team grows.

**If you mostly need design mockup review rather than bug tracking**, Ruttl's side-by-side comparison feature is closer to what you actually need than a full bug tracker.

**If you're a small team just getting started and want to test the waters for free**, Userback or Atarim's free tiers let you try the workflow before committing to a paid plan - just know you'll likely outgrow them if your team scales past a handful of people.
